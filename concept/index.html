Stage 1

Discovery & Collection

Gathering initial literature, raw data, surveys, or field observations. The earliest ingestion of raw signals and leads.

Stage 2

Structuring & Processing

Categorizing and contextualizing the inputs. Coding qualitative data, scrubbing quantitative datasets, and organizing sources into a literature review.

Stage 3

Hypothesis & Concept

Defining the core research question, formulating a thesis, and designing the methodological strategy and blueprint.

Stage 4

Execution & Investigation

Running the actual experiments, conducting interviews, or running models. Active intelligence gathering and probing the theory.

Stage 5

Synthesis & Verification

Analyzing the findings, proving or disproving the hypothesis, and peer-reviewing to mitigate risk and bias.

Stage 6

Deployment & Action

Publishing the paper, presenting findings, or handing the research over to decision-makers. Finalizing the output.
